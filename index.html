1. Workshop Description

Title: Democratizing GenAI and Scalable NLP with HiPC for Societal Impact

Call for Papers

Authors are invited to submit original, unpublished research papers or work-in-progress contributions related to, but not limited to, the following topics:

  • HPC Architectures and Frameworks for NLP and GenAI
  • HPC-enabled GenAI in low-resource settings
  • Generative AI Applications for Societal Domains
  • Data-centric and Resource-efficient AI
  • Scalable NLP Pipelines and Tooling
  • Responsible and Inclusive GenAI
  • Low-resource and Multilingual NLP
  • Case Studies and Field Deployments

Primary Goals

The primary goals of the workshop are to:

  • Bring together experts from HPC, NLP, and Generative AI communities to address the unique computational and linguistic challenges in developing scalable solutions for global languages.
  • Promote research and development of efficient training, inference, and deployment methods for GenAI and NLP that can handle the diversity and scale of the multilingual ecosystem.
  • Showcase and discuss practical use cases of GenAI and NLP in critical areas such as agriculture, education, healthcare, governance, rural development and sustainability.

Workshop Overview

The rise of Generative AI and Natural Language Processing has transformed the way information is generated, accessed, and utilized. However, harnessing these technologies at scale for global languages and real-world societal applications remains a significant challenge due to computational, linguistic, and resource barriers. This workshop aims to bring together researchers, practitioners, and policymakers to explore how High Performance Computing (HPC) can enable scalable, efficient, and inclusive GenAI and NLP solutions for societal impact areas such as agriculture, education, healthcare, governance, rural development and sustainability.

The workshop will provide a platform to share cutting-edge research, practical deployments, and collaborative initiatives that democratize access to advanced language technologies for communities across India and other linguistically diverse regions. By connecting NLP, GenAI, and HPC experts with domain stakeholders, the workshop will identify research gaps, foster collaborations, and advance the mission of using AI responsibly and equitably for societal good. We welcome contributions covering novel algorithms, system architectures, datasets, and case studies demonstrating scalable and impactful applications of GenAI and NLP.

4. Submission and Review Strategy (for workshops with proceedings)

The workshop will solicit high-quality research and position papers through an open call for papers, announced via the HiPC conference channels, relevant mailing lists, and academic networks. Submissions will be accepted in standard double-column IEEE format, and authors will be encouraged to submit original, work in progress, unpublished work that aligns with the workshop's theme of scalable NLP, Generative AI, and societal applications using HPC.

Each submitted paper will undergo a rigorous peer-review process, with at least three independent reviewers per paper drawn from the workshop's technical program committee and external experts. The review will assess the novelty, technical soundness, relevance to the workshop goals, and potential impact. To maintain high quality and topical relevance, the organizers will provide clear review guidelines, ensure constructive feedback, and strike a careful balance between theoretical and applied contributions, while adhering to the planned timeline.
